Understanding Phishing Attacks

What is phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime in which a target or targets are contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking and credit card details, and passwords.

In simpler terms, phishing is a cyber attack where the attacker tricks the target into disclosing personal information, revealing login credentials, or transferring money.

Why should I be concerned about phishing?

The purpose of phishing is to collect sensitive information with the intention of using that information to gain access to otherwise protected data, networks, etc. A phisher's success is contingent upon establishing trust with its victims.

Successful Phishing attacks can:

  • Cause financial loss for victims
  • Put their personal information at risk
  • Put data and systems at risk

Types of Phishing Attacks

Deceptive Phishing

Fraudsters impersonate a legitimate company to steal personal data or login credentials using threats and urgency to scare users.

Spear Phishing

Targets specific individuals instead of a wide group, often researching victims on social media to customize communications.

Smishing

SMS phishing where attackers send fraudulent text messages to gather information like credit card numbers or passwords.

Vishing

Voice phishing combines VoIP with phishing, involving calls from fraudulent persons attempting to obtain sensitive information.

How to Protect Yourself

Use strong passwords and be wary of posting personal details on social media

Never give away personal information in an email or unsolicited call

Beware of urgent or time-sensitive warnings - phishing attacks often create false urgency

Verify emails by contacting the organization directly through official channels

About PhishSecure

Phishing is an internet scam in which an attacker sends out fake messages that look to come from a trusted source. A URL or file will be included in the mail, which when clicked will steal personal information or infect a computer with a virus.

PhishSecure is a project which aims to help reduce phishing attacks by helping internet users authenticate URL links by testing if they are phishing or legitimate. The process of validating a Website URL for phishing or legitimate has gone through several Machine learning models.

Real Phishing Attack Cases

Learn from these real-world examples of major phishing attacks

01

John Podesta's Email

One of the most notable phishing attacks was on John Podesta, chairman of Hillary Clinton's presidential campaign. Russian hackers sent an email pretending to be Google, claiming he needed to change his password after an attempted hack. When someone with access to his account used the compromised link, hackers gained access and released thousands of emails via WikiLeaks.

02

BenefitMall Data Breach

Between June and October 2018, BenefitMall's website was accessed via employee email credentials exposed during a phishing attack. The breach potentially exposed names, email addresses, birth dates, bank account information, and insurance details of thousands of customers from over 200,000 small and medium-sized businesses.

03

Sony Pictures Attack

In retaliation for the movie "The Interview," North Korean hackers launched a devastating attack on Sony Pictures in November 2014. Using phishing emails containing malware, the attackers gained network access, threatened employees, stole confidential data, and disabled thousands of computers, costing the company over $100 million.
